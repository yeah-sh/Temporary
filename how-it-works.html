How It Works

LocalDeliv is a community-driven delivery app that connects local businesses and individuals to delivery drivers from the same community, fostering an eco-friendly and efficient delivery network. Here's how it works:

Users

The platform serves three primary groups:

  1. Senders (Customers) – Individuals or businesses who need items delivered, such as groceries, food, or packages.
  2. Drivers (Delivery Partners) – Local community members who perform deliveries. They use their personal vehicles or other modes of transportation and operate as independent contractors.
  3. Admin (Platform) – Platform administrators ensure smooth operation, handle payment processing, and resolve disputes.

App Structure

  1. Request Interface – Customers specify the details of the delivery, including pickup and drop-off locations, time, and any special instructions.
  2. Matching System – The app matches delivery requests with nearby available drivers using an algorithm.
  3. Tracking and Notifications – Customers can track their delivery in real time and communicate with the driver.
  4. Payment Processing – Payments are handled directly through the app, with fees and tips based on distance, time, or complexity.
  5. Rating and Feedback – Both customers and drivers can rate their experience, helping maintain quality and accountability on the platform.

Operational Workflow

  1. Customer Places an Order – A customer initiates a request by selecting the items to be delivered, providing pickup and delivery addresses, and adding any special instructions.
  2. Driver Accepts the Request – Available drivers receive the request and can choose to accept the delivery. The app may assign drivers based on proximity or rating.
  3. Pick-Up and Delivery – Once accepted, the driver picks up the item(s) and delivers them to the specified location.
  4. Real-Time Tracking – Customers track the driver’s progress via GPS for transparency and convenience.
  5. Completion and Payment – After successful delivery, the payment is processed automatically. Drivers are compensated either per delivery or through an agreed-upon structure.
  6. Feedback Loop – Both the customer and the driver provide feedback, helping improve the service for future deliveries.
